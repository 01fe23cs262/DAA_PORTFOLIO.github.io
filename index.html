Unit ‚Äì 1

Chapter 1: Fundamentals of Algorithms and Problem Solving

This chapter introduces the fundamentals of algorithms, exploring space and time complexities, which are critical for analyzing algorithm efficiency. It revisits data structures like stacks and queues and dives into recursion, comparing it with iteration. Additionally, it explains concepts like backtracking and recursive functions, including examples like the Towers of Hanoi problem.

Chapter 2: Hashing and Hash Tables

The chapter focuses on efficient data retrieval using hashing techniques. It explains direct address tables and hash tables, detailing the design of hash functions and strategies for collision resolution. Practical applications highlight how hashing is used to speed up data access in real-world scenarios.

Chapter 3: Graphs and Trees

This chapter covers fundamental data structures like graphs and trees, emphasizing their computer representation. It includes traversal techniques (DFS, BFS) and advanced trees such as AVL and 2-3 trees. Applications of binary trees, such as Tries, are discussed, along with real-world use cases in data organization and searching.

Chapter 4: Sorting Techniques

The chapter provides a comprehensive overview of sorting algorithms, including bubble sort, selection sort, and insertion sort for basic understanding. Advanced methods like merge sort, quick sort, and heap sort are explained in terms of their efficiency and use cases. Visual examples help clarify how these algorithms operate.

Chapter 5: Substring Search Algorithms

This chapter delves into substring search techniques, starting with the brute-force approach. Advanced algorithms like Boyer-Moore, Knuth-Morris-Pratt, and Rabin-Karp are explained, showcasing their efficiency in text searching and applications in fields like bioinformatics and plagiarism detection.

Chapter 6: Graph Algorithms

Graph algorithms play a critical role in problem-solving.
